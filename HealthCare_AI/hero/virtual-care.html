Quick steps to help in emergencies before reaching a hospital.

Snake Bite

Keep patient calm, tie above bite (not too tight), rush to hospital. Do not cut or suck.

Burns

Cool with running water, no oil/ghee. Cover with clean cloth.

Diarrhea

Give ORS/salt-sugar water. Keep hydrated. If severe, visit hospital.

Cuts & Bleeding

Wash, press with cloth, bandage. Get tetanus injection if deep.

Dog Bite

Wash with soap & water 15 mins, then visit hospital for rabies vaccine.

Fever

Keep hydrated, rest. Use paracetamol. See doctor if persists.

Choking

If child chokes, bend forward and pat back strongly between shoulder blades.

Heat Stroke

Move to cool place, sponge with wet cloth, give sips of water.

Asthma Attack

Help patient sit upright, use inhaler if available, rush to hospital if severe.

Basic health awareness to keep rural families safe and healthy.

Clean Drinking Water

Boil/filter water before drinking to prevent diarrhea & cholera.

Maternal Care

Pregnant women should visit health centers regularly & take iron tablets.

Vaccination

Ensure children are fully vaccinated against polio, measles, etc.

Nutrition

Eat balanced meals with vegetables, grains & pulses.

Hygiene

Wash hands before meals & after using toilet to prevent infections.

Oral Health

Brush teeth twice daily, avoid chewing tobacco & smoking.
